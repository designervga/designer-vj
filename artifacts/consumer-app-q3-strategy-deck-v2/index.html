More liquidity creates the next problem: relevance

Q1 and Q2 added more stores and products to show. Q3 has to decide which ones are relevant enough to make someone buy.

Supply liquidity is less constrained than relevance.

Autofollow, tracking, Related Products, and campaign supply create enough places to surface products and stores.

Ranking becomes the bottleneck.

Deals, Favorite Stores, Reviews, Clips, coupons, and Related Products only work if the user trusts the recommendation.

The strategic shift is not launching more shopping surfaces. It is proving matching quality across the inventory we can now surface.
Q3 bets organized by demand intent and liquidity or relevance.

Ranking V0 is the decision system behind low-intent discovery

Ranking V0 turns liquidity into a measurable recommendation system: score eligible stores and products, explain each impression, and compare outcomes by surface.

Start explainable.

Use store affinity, product quality, offer quality, merchant quality, and eligibility before heavier personalization.

Gate 1: 100% of ranked impressions log score version and reason code.
Gate 2: 80%+ of eligible stores and products receive enough ranking inputs to be scored; 90%+ stretch.
Gate 3: Low-intent CTR +25% and click-to-purchase +15% without higher opt-out or weaker click-to-purchase quality.
Gate 4: New-to-store orders reach 2x Q2 baseline or 15% to 25% of attributed orders from ranked discovery.

Decision needed: prove relevance before expanding commerce scope

Approve Q3 as a relevance-validation quarter: ship high-intent recovery, pilot ranked discovery, measure new demand, and keep native commerce outside scope.

Approve

Two workstreams, one proof.

High Intent Recovery captures existing demand. Low Intent Discovery validates whether ranking can create habit and new-to-store orders.

  • Keep Q3 focused on relevance.
  • Use first-purchase coupons where store incentives already exist.
  • Run Deals as a bounded pilot.
Measure

Do not wait for GMV alone.

Decision quality comes from D30 recurrence, uninstall, discoverer WAU, CTR, click-to-purchase, attribution mix, and new-to-store orders.

  • 100% of ranked impressions log score version and reason code.
  • New-to-store as the demand proof.
  • Q3 ambition, not forecast.
Protect

Preserve merchant storefronts.

Q3 should not absorb PDP, cart, or checkout. The app should qualify demand and send the buyer back to the merchant experience.

  • No native PDP, cart, or checkout.
  • No increase in opt-out or low-quality traffic.
  • No marketplace-like catalog expansion.
Risks

Baseline gaps are the first blocker.

The plan needs clear denominators before rollout so the team can separate product learning from attribution noise.

  • Coupon coverage by eligible store.
  • Deals merchant opt-in supply target.
  • Ranked discovery attribution split.
The tradeoff is explicit: delay larger commerce scope until the app proves recommendation relevance and retained demand.

Appendix: high-intent benchmark and next-horizon boundaries

High-intent recovery explains the near-term conversion bet. Next-horizon ideas only earn priority if Q3 proves relevance.

Abandoned Cart has much stronger click-to-purchase than lower-intent discovery. That is why High Intent Recovery stays in plan while Low Intent Discovery is being proven.

Boundary

Personal Shopper is a next-horizon layer, not a Q3 commitment.

It should sit on top of primitives Q3 is trying to prove: curated supply, ranking, offer detail, attribution, and direct-to-store handoff.

Keep in-app PDP, cart, and checkout outside Q3 until recommendation quality is proven.
Keep Wallet Identity Memory outside Q3 unless the relevance proof layer is ready.
Do not prioritize AI engagement by itself. Demand proof comes first.
