Stores, products, carts, offers and purchase context that can legally and safely enter the Home.

2

Known stores, similar stores, products in stock, discounts and high-quality fallback supply.

3

4

Slate rules

The PM question

Do we have enough good candidates for this person, in this shelf?

If yes, rank them. If not, expand with similar stores, quality-floor products or controlled exploration.

The highest-value quick win is store-first ranking.

The current Home already has a strong relationship surface: store avatars and "Suas marcas". That is where personalization becomes visible with the least product risk.

Why this surface first

Users understand stores as a personal object. They bought, followed, visited or left carts there. Reordering stores is easy to explain and easy to validate.

What changes for the consumer

The Home stops feeling like a generic catalog. The first stores are familiar, recent, trustworthy and likely to have useful products.

What stays simple

Carts, current orders, recently viewed products and buy-again can stay recency-led in the MVP. They are already intent-heavy.

The MVP has two rankings, not one giant feed.

First rank stores for the user. Then rank products inside each selected store card.

README for PMs

What we are building

A store-first personalization layer for the Consumer App Home. It should improve discovery without becoming a marketplace feed, paid auction or opaque ML system.

explainable direct-to-store content-based first ML later

Operating principles

Start narrow Personalize stores and products inside store cards before attempting full Home optimization.
Keep intent Orders, carts and recently viewed products can stay recency-led until ranking logs prove otherwise.
Use content Verticals, categories, tags, price bands and store metadata are enough for a useful V0.
Log the slate Every ranked item needs section, rank position, reason code and score version.

The implementation path.

V0 should create consumer value now and make the next ML step safer.

MVP

Store-first ranking

Rank the store avatar strip and "Suas marcas". Rank products inside each selected store card. Use content-based affinity, store quality, recency, delivery fit and product quality.

Next

Broaden product shelves

Apply the same scoring and slate rules to "Based on purchases", discounts, launches and popular products, with careful diversity controls.

Later

Retrieval and collaborative learning

Use embeddings as candidate generators and collaborative filtering only after impressions, rank positions, reason codes and score versions are reliable.

Recommended decision

Ship an explainable V0 that makes stores feel personal first.

The biggest near-term lift is not smarter abandoned carts or recently viewed products. It is making the stores and products already present in the Home appear in a more useful order for each consumer.
