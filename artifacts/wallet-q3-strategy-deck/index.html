Q3 must prove Wallet turns exposure into reliable GMV adoption

The quarter is not about more reach. It is about proving that recognized checkout creates merchant conversion, GPV concentration and safer adoption at scale.

Wallet already has the structural right to win

It owns portable identity, saved payment data, checkout acceleration and the Nuvem Pago / Pago Nube path in one system. Q3 has to turn that advantage into measurable merchant value.

No-CVV is the highest-conviction buyer-facing conversion bet

The question moved from whether no-CVV works to how we scale it safely. The lift is real in saved-card flows, but rollout depends on risk orchestration and provider correctness.

What Q3 can now build on

+3.13 pp BR saved-card cart approval, 89.33% to 92.46%, 95% CI +0.93 to +5.35 pp.
~+4 pp AR Pago Nube approval lift, enough to stop extending the test.
34s CVV adds 28% of total checkout time in saved-card flows.
1.3% saved-card payments fail due to invalid CVV.

What must be true before scale

Risk decisioning: TMX provides device/session signals and Neblina decides when to skip or require CVV.
User-facing fallback: if antifraud needs CVV, Wallet asks after the payment attempt instead of creating a silent rejection path.
Provider contract: installments, authorization state and PSP payloads must reconcile with UI and internal records.
Delayed guardrail: chargebacks lag 30 to 90 days, so scale needs staged exposure, issuer/acquirer monitoring and a kill switch.
Readout discipline: approval, fraud, retries and generic rejection buckets are separate signals.
Scope note: no-CVV metrics are limited to saved-card payment flows and should not be read as global checkout CVR uplift.

Parity debt needs capability-level routing, not generic support

Every Core-only capability needs an explicit routing policy. Q3 should prevent the fast path from becoming a worse checkout for stores with benefits or complex payment cases.

Trigger the decision

New UI, modal, input, selector or message in Core.
New total, discount, value instrument or payment-combination rule.
Provider, 3DS, iframe, redirect or PSP-specific behavior changes.
Store/account capability becomes a migration blocker.

Choose one policy

1

Support in EC

Build equivalent UI, state, validation, summary, payload and monitoring.

2

Route to Core

Keep buyer in the supported checkout path and make the fallback reason visible.

3

Mark ineligible

Do not show EC for affected stores, carts, users, apps or PSPs.

Protect the readout

Gift Card and Store Credit can change totals and benefits.
Subscriptions and recurring payments need a separate card-token contract.
Kits and bundles need an equivalent line-item representation.
B2B and international cases stay outside the default path until scoped.
Scope note: this is a strategic routing policy, not a commitment to support every Core capability in EC during Q3.
Q3 should prove adoption quality before claiming North Star movement

The readout should stay simple. Wallet needs three leading proofs before Merchant CVR Uplift becomes a credible Q3 story.

Proof 1

Conversion improves without hiding friction.

No-CVV approval holds beyond tests, while EC started-to-paid conversion becomes instrumented instead of inferred.

Proof 2

Adoption grows with completion quality.

EC moves from the ~8.5% draft read toward the ~40% ambition only if fallback reasons and NP/PN mix stay healthy.

Proof 3

Foundations are ready before scale.

Login persistence, OTP pressure, provider contracts, TMX, Neblina and monitoring become rollout gates.

Do not declare North Star impact until the guardrails hold.

Decision: approve the scope, protect the no-gos, and measure readiness before scale

The alignment ask is not "ship everything." It is to approve the few bets that can prove Wallet's merchant value and keep the default path honest.

Approve

Q3 should lead with no-CVV, single primary checkout CTA and EC UI as the conversion bets.

Passwordless remains enablement.
Foundations stay attached to rollout gates.

Protect

Do not expand EC ownership into every Core Checkout edge case by default.

No-gos remain explicit.
Unsupported cases route intentionally.

Measure

Read leading proof before declaring Merchant CVR Uplift.

EC usage plus started-to-paid conversion.
No-CVV quality plus fallback reasons.

Trade off

Defaulting EC increases strategic leverage and operational risk at the same time.

If conversion does not hold, route back.
If risk is not ready, no-CVV stays staged.
